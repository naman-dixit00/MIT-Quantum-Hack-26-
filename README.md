## Executive Summary

MIT iQuHACK (Interdisciplinary Quantum Hackathon) is Massachusetts Institute of Technology’s premier annual quantum computing hackathon, serving as a global nexus for advanced quantum research, algorithmic development, and hardware validation. Bringing together a diverse cohort ranging from advanced graduate researchers to classical computing engineers, the platform challenges participants to design, optimize, and deploy novel quantum workflows on state-of-the-art quantum hardware and simulators.

The 2026 edition (seventh iteration), held from January 30 to February 1, 2026, focused heavily on scalable quantum architectures and hybrid classical-quantum optimization models. This repository serves as the definitive engineering log, source code archive, and theoretical documentation for the deployment developed during the BlueQubit challenge track.
